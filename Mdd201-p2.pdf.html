Written Mirror  
Forget & Recall  
Concept  
Once I had lost my ﬂash disk with important information and works in. The only thing I could remember is  
that I did plug it on to the computer several hours ago. I wonder why there isn’t any system for reminding of  
taking the disk before leaving or to position where the disk is. Then I realised that the reminding system can  
only be sometimes partly useful because there are still countless people being late though they set alarms to  
wake them up a few hours ago before the deadline.  
People have been keeping forgetting things important to them since they were born, mostly for laziness and  
oblivion. Their books, their ﬂash disks, their keys and their massive various passwords to every software  
account —— or why there is a “forgot password” button on every login webpage? However, when they try to  
recall, the only things they can make sure is the scene happened a few minutes or seconds ago before they set  
their codes. It seems that the Internet emphasizes the limited capability of human Hippocampus. Memories for  
us are like a mirror that has been written with ﬁngers which seems that nothing is there. Once the water vapour appears  
again, all the words written before are revealed. Sometimes things come out clearly, sometimes really blurred.  
To celebrate this global style that have been set and passing down for thousand years, and  
let more people remember what they lost, the project ‘Written Mirror’ was set.
